The BitTorrent protocol itself

I like torrents mostly because the BitTorrent protocol revolutionized the Internet. It was created when your average internet connection was around 50 KB/s, and most people still used dial-up. But why it was so revolutional?

First, it is very hard to shut down a torrent network - that's why it is so commonly used for piracy and rare / lost media archival. Back in the day most websites were owned by people, not companies. A side effect of this was that servers of a website could be shut down at any time due to many reasons. The creator of that site could be bored, forgot to turn on his server etc. Obviously, when the servers are down, you cannot download stuff from this person. BitTorrent is decentralized, so everyone having the file on his own computer is a potential server. Only one person online (more popular torrents have even 50 people at the same time) can keep the torrent up and send the data to you. Because of this decentralization, most companies can't stop even a single torrent.

Aside from that, download speeds of the files sent through torrent weren't capped by the server, so these files could be downloaded with very high speeds. These reasons are all i need to prefer torrent over anything else.
